01The Big Idea

Governance that stays private — and acts on its own.

Today, on-chain voting is a glass box: everyone sees who voted, how, and how much they hold — and whoever holds the most can watch the live tally and swing it at the last second. Then, even after a vote passes, a human still has to manually sign off to make anything happen. ShadowKit removes both problems at once.

Private & sealed

Zero-knowledge proofs hide identity, choice, and weight. Timelock encryption keeps the tally invisible until close.

Autonomous

When voting closes, an AI agent reads the result and executes the on-chain action itself — no human approval needed.

Unbreakable

The agent's spending is gated on-chain. Even a hallucinating AI literally cannot move funds the vote didn't approve.

In one sentence

ShadowKit lets any DAO vote in complete privacy, keeps the outcome sealed until the deadline, and lets an autonomous agent carry out the decision — all secured by math, not middlemen.

It ships as infrastructure — an SDK and middleware any Stellar project can adopt — not a one-off app. The sample treasury, ShadowDAO, is just the showroom.

02The Problem

Web3 voting is public, gameable, and slow to act.

Three cracks in how decentralized communities decide and execute today — each one a reason people don't participate, or don't trust the outcome.

The glass-box ballot

On-chain votes are public by default. Small holders are exposed to whales, rival DAOs, and analytics firms who can see — and pressure — every choice. So many simply don't vote at all.

The bandwagon trap

When the running tally is visible, a whale can wait, watch, and swing the result at the last second. Live results invite last-minute manipulation and herd behavior — the loudest money wins.

The 3am multisig

Even after a vote passes, execution waits on humans to manually co-sign a multisig. It's slow, centralized, and fragile — a single missing signer can stall a decision the community already made.

The result: low participation, manipulable outcomes, and execution that depends on the very middlemen decentralization was meant to remove.

03The Solution

Three pillars, one autonomous loop.

Each pillar maps to a hackathon track — and together they make a vote that is private, fair, and self-executing. Plain-language first, real technology underneath.

PILLAR 01Hack Privacy

Private, sealed voting

Like a sealed ballot box the organizers themselves can't peek into — and no one can see who's winning until the timer hits zero. Identity, choice, and voting weight all stay hidden.

Under the hood: Groth16 zk-SNARKs verified on-chain (BLS12-381) · nullifiers stop double-voting · drand timelock encryption seals the tally until close.

PILLAR 02Hack Agentic

Autonomous agent execution

The community decides; a tireless robot treasurer carries it out the instant voting closes — picking the best way to execute within the approved budget. No multisig phone-tree at 3am.

Under the hood: LLM-planned (Google Gemini) slippage / routing / timing · session-key signing · pays per-call for live market data via x402.

PILLAR 03The Safety Lock

The math lock

Even if the AI hallucinates or goes rogue, the blockchain itself refuses any move the community didn't approve — wrong amount, wrong target, wrong asset, or a vote that never passed.

Under the hood: an OpenZeppelin Smart Account policy enforces approved + capped + single-shot + correct target/asset on every transaction.

04The Demo Story · "Hero Loop"

One proposal, start to finish.

The exact flow that runs live in the demo — the narrative spine for a pitch deck. A treasury proposal goes from a private vote to a fully autonomous on-chain swap, with no human ever touching the execution.

1

Connect

A member signs in with a passkey — no seed phrase, no extension friction. Face or fingerprint, done.

2

Proposal goes live

"Swap 15,000 USDC → XLM" opens with a voting deadline. A countdown starts ticking.

3

Private vote

In the browser, a zero-knowledge proof is built and the vote is timelock-encrypted to the deadline. Choice and weight never leak.

4

Sealed on-chain

The contract verifies each proof and stores the sealed vote. The UI shows only "N votes cast · results hidden" + countdown. No one can see who's winning.

5

Reveal at the deadline

The drand beacon unlocks the votes. The weighted tally is computed and verified on-chain — the first time anyone sees the result.

6

The agent acts

On quorum, the AI agent buys market data, plans the swap within the approved cap, and signs. The on-chain policy checks every condition before allowing it.

7

Done — on-chain & live

The swap executes on a Stellar DEX; the treasury updates in real time; AgentBoard's terminal streams the agent's reasoning. No human ever approved the transaction.

09Cheat Sheet

Plain-language glossary & fast facts.

So you're never lost in a technical conversation. Everything you need to speak about ShadowKit with confidence.

Glossary
Zero-knowledge proof (ZK)
Prove something is true without revealing the secret — e.g. "I'm eligible and my vote is valid" without showing who you are or how you voted.
Timelock encryption
A digital lock that can only be opened after a set time. Used to seal the vote tally until the deadline.
Nullifier
A one-time anonymous "I voted" token that prevents double-voting without revealing who voted.
Quorum
The minimum participation and agreement needed for a proposal to officially pass.
Soroban
Stellar's smart-contract platform — the on-chain programs that enforce the rules.
The agent
An AI program that executes the approved decision on-chain, strictly bounded by policy.
Smart-account policy
The on-chain rulebook that mathematically limits what the agent is allowed to spend.
x402
A protocol for tiny pay-per-call payments — how the agent buys live market data.
