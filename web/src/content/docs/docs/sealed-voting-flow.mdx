## The seven steps

<Steps>

1. **Snapshot eligible holders** â€” build a Poseidon Merkle tree; its root is registered on `GovVault` at init.

2. **Create a proposal** â€” `create_proposal(action_spec, cap, deadline)` returns a proposal id; the deadline fixes the tlock round.

3. **Prove + seal a vote** â€” `generateVoteProof`: Groth16 proof + tlock-sealed `(direction, weight)`. _The witness never leaves the browser._

4. **Cast on-chain** â€” `cast_vote` verifies the proof, checks the nullifier + binding + root, and stores the sealed ciphertext. **No tally is exposed.**

5. **Sealed until close** â€” `votes_cast` is public; `weighted_yes/no` stay None. The tally is cryptographically unknowable until the drand round.

6. **Close & reveal** â€” after the deadline, `buildRevealArgs` tlock-decrypts every vote; `close_and_reveal` re-aggregates on-chain â†’ `Approved | Rejected`.

7. **Agent executes** â€” on approval, the bounded agent pays for data, plans, and submits a policy-gated swap; `mark_executed` makes it single-shot.

</Steps>

## 3â€“4 Â· Prove, seal, and cast

The proof and the sealed ciphertext are produced together so the proof attests the ciphertext is well-formed (public signal #4).


## 5 Â· Sealed until close

The running tally is unknowable: `weighted_yes` / `weighted_no` are `None` in `ProposalView`, and the votes themselves are tlock-encrypted to a drand round that hasn't happened yet. Only `votes_cast` (participation) is public.

`close_and_reveal` requires one `VoteDecryption` per stored sealed vote, in the same order, each carrying its `sealedCommitmentHash`. The contract recomputes the weighted sums itself â€” you cannot substitute or reorder votes.
